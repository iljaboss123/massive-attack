Harmony & Pitch

Unlike rhythm and Timbre, which change a lot throughout their songs, harmony in many Massive Attack songs is often subtle and repetitive, but always very crucial for shaping the atmosphere of the songs. Across the three tracks it is shown that harmony is not used as a very complex or dynamic way, but more as a good foundation for the rhythm and texture in the songs. With Five Man Army not being a very melodic song you see a very continuous and repetitive harmonic structure, showing the loop based sample they used in their earlier works. Dissolved Girl introduces more contrast and variation in the harmony, showing a change from the simple rhythm focused sound of Blue Lines. Listening to the song you hear the dark and slow rising atmosphere that is reflected by the chromagram. Paradise Circus shows even clearer harmonic patterns, with the repetition and spacing being more defined. Listening to the song you will hear the same repetitive keys throughout the song, showing Massive Attack mastered their simple, repetitive sound here while still making interesting and critically acclaimed music. Overall, this shows that Massive Attack does not rely on complex chord progressions, but uses harmony to create interesting sonic landscapes to support atmosphere and genre blending.
